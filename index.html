Supercharge Project


Altius institute has world class TALEN and TALE-TF production capabilities. TALENs are transcription activator like effector nucleases, which are restriction enzymes that are designed to cut DNA with very high specificity. TALEs are delivered into cells through electroporation, electric shock that momentarily blows holes in cells, so the TALE mRNA can enter or through the use of retroviral vectors. Electroporation is harsh on the cells leading to lower cell viability and some changes in cellular physiology. Retroviral vectors can be limited in their efficacy as there are carrying capacity constriants and synthesis of these viruses can be difficult.


Altius is working toward the development of reagents that will allow for the delivery of TALEs through the employment of supercharge principles. Supercharging is a technique developed by the Liu Lab at Harvard University. This technique allows for transportation of reagents across the cell membrane through the user of highly charged moieties.
